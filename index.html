Welcome to Open* for ICTD

Information and Communication Technology for Development ("ICTD"), also known as Information and Communications Technologies for Development or "ICT4D", refers to the use of Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs) in the fields of socioeconomic development, international development and human rights. The theory behind this is that more and better information and communication furthers the development of a society.[1]

Open* for ICTD is a practical, open system framework supporting an integrated approach to challenges faced by ICTD. It provides a foundation to build upon and a toolkit to get started. Open* for ICTD's integrated approach supports the collaboration of practitioners with distinct expertise, such as an ICTD practioner and a researcher or scientist. Open* for ICTD also encourages the development of integrative practitioners, given the realities of many ICTD projects such as small teams.

Objectives

The Open* for ICTD framework's initial objective is to increase access to knowledge.

Technology breaks down barriers to access, but the establish norm in academia and research, is to erect paywalls around knowledge.

The Open* for ICTD establishes a framework that will not only provide access to knowledge, but enable this by leveraging Open Source and Open Standards-based technologies that are not made inaccessible by licensing costs.
